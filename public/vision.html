Product roadmap

Vision

Build decision infrastructure for institutional complexity — guidance that is auditable, policy-aware, and resilient across scale.

Strategic direction

From assistant to infrastructure

OmanX is not positioned as a conversational novelty. It is a guidance layer that structures institutional decisions and preserves reasoning quality over time.

Institutional-grade decision support

The long-term system supports scholars, sponsors, and advisory teams with shared, traceable decision records and explicit constraint handling.

Scalable and auditable guidance

As adoption grows, OmanX will provide confidence tracking, decision provenance, and robust policy update workflows.

Phases
Phase 1 · Now

Single-user decision workspace

Structured outputs, local continuity, policy-aware framing for individual Omani scholars navigating US institutions.

Phase 2 · Next

Multi-user collaboration layer

Shared rooms, thread boards, advisor coordination, and visible status signals across student cohorts.

Phase 3 · Future

Institutional-grade infrastructure

Decision provenance, confidence tracking, MoHE policy synchronization, and formal escalation workflows for sponsors and advisors.

What changes before institutional scale

Policy registry

Move from static policy entries toward versioned policy records with source URLs, effective dates, review status, and owners.

Evaluation harness

Maintain a test set of high-risk scholar questions and grade answers for correctness, caution, source use, and escalation quality before each major release.

Advisor workflow

Introduce queues, notes, exports, and role boundaries only after real scholar usage proves which cases need human follow-up.

Pilot philosophy

Start with usefulness, earn authority

The near-term goal is not to present OmanX as an official decision maker. The goal is to reduce confusion for scholars, document common question patterns, and show institutions where support demand is concentrated.

Authority should be earned through source audits, evaluation results, partner feedback, and a clear handoff model for questions that require official confirmation.
