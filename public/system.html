System documentation

System

OmanX is a decision infrastructure layer for Omani scholars managing institutional constraints across admissions, sponsorship, compliance, and academic progression.

Capabilities

University pathway guidance

Transforms fragmented application and enrollment questions into ordered decision sequences, deadlines, and escalation points.

Sponsorship and scholarship constraints

Maps scholarship terms, sponsor requirements, and academic obligations into explicit constraints so recommendations remain policy-aligned.

Visa and compliance context

Flags compliance-sensitive topics and outputs conservative guidance with verification boundaries and accountable offices.

Structured decision outputs

Every response is organized into a decision artifact with constraints, interpretation, recommended next step, and stated assumptions.

How the system works

Destination routing

OmanX detects whether a question belongs to the United States, United Kingdom, or Australia, then combines that destination dataset with shared MoHE scholarship rules.

Risk classification

Everyday questions stay fast and conversational. Visa, work, scholarship, insurance, legal, academic standing, and housing disputes move through stricter compliance handling.

Source layering

Saved OmanX policy entries provide structure. Live official-source search is used for compliance topics when enabled, especially where deadlines or current rules may change.

Current boundaries

Local-first history

Chat history is stored in the browser today. This keeps the beta lightweight and private, but it also means history does not automatically follow a student across devices.

Human confirmation remains required

OmanX can help a scholar organize facts, identify likely offices, and avoid obvious risky steps. It does not replace a DSO, university advisor, MoHE representative, attorney, doctor, or official government source.
